**Case Presentation - Stage 1**

An 83-year-old woman presents with fatigue and unintentional weight loss over the past six weeks. She also reports a dry cough during this period but denies fevers, chills, or night sweats.

Her medical history is significant for acute myeloid leukemia (AML) diagnosed 30 months ago, which evolved from polycythemia vera. She was treated with the IDH2 inhibitor enasidenib for 24 months but experienced a relapse and is currently on the FLT3 inhibitor gilteritinib. Additionally, she was diagnosed with psoriasis two months ago.

She was born in Germany and has lived in the United States for the past 50 years. She does not use tobacco. Her medications include cyclosporine and ustekinumab; however, she has had indeterminate interferon-gamma release assay (IGRA) results twice.

Allergies: None.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 99.7°F (37.6°C)
- Heart Rate: 87 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 117/82 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 15 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 96% on room air

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** The patient appears comfortable and in no acute distress.
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rate and rhythm; no murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
- **Pulmonary:** Crackles are heard in the right lower lung field.
- **Abdominal:** Non-tender and non-distended.
- **Neurological:** No focal deficits observed.
- **Extremities/Skin:** No edema; skin examination unremarkable.
