

**Chief Complaint:**
Rectal urgency.

**History of Present Illness:**
A 33-year-old male presents with rectal urgency. Two weeks ago, he experienced diarrhea with increased frequency, which was presumed to be viral in origin. Since then, the diarrhea has mostly subsided. He now reports an ache in the rectum that began after the viral symptoms resolved. The pain worsens with sitting. He goes to the bathroom around 20 times a day. Each time, he passes approximately 75 grams of stool, which is of Bristol Stool Scale types 3 to 4.

He describes a sensation of needing to pass stool frequently but passes only normal-formed stools in small amounts. He denies blood in the stool, fever, weight loss, or changes in appetite.

**Past Medical History:**
- No significant gastrointestinal history.

**Medications:**
- Over-the-counter ibuprofen.
- Fiber supplements.

**Family History:**
- Not provided.

**Social History:**
- Not provided.

**Allergies:**
- No known drug allergies.

**Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: Within normal limits.
- Heart Rate: Within normal limits.
- Blood Pressure: Within normal limits.
- Respiratory Rate: Within normal limits.

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Alert, well-appearing male in no acute distress.
- **Abdominal Examination:** Soft, non-tender, no abdominal tenderness or distension.
- **Rectal Examination:**
  - Tenderness in the levator ani muscle.
  - Sphincter tone is normal.
  - No masses or hemorrhoids are appreciated.
  - Patient exhibits tenesmus (a sensation of incomplete evacuation).
