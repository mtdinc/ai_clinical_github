**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
A 65-year-old female presents with a one-week history of right flank pain.

**History of Present Illness:**
The patient reports that approximately one week ago, she began experiencing a tingling pain on her right flank. She describes the pain as gradual in onset, with a tingling quality, and rates it as 6 out of 10 in intensity. The pain is somewhat similar to an episode of herpes zoster (shingles) she had 15 years ago on her lower left back. The discomfort has caused difficulty sleeping at night. There is no radiation of the pain. Use of a heating pad provided no relief. Deep breathing and physical activity do not exacerbate the pain.

**Past Medical History:**

- Hypertension, well-controlled
- Hyperlipidemia
- Previous episode of herpes zoster on the lower left back, 15 years ago

**Medications:**

- Losartan 25 mg daily
- Atorvastatin 10 mg daily
- No new medications in the past year

**Social History:**

- Lives with her husband
- Worked as a farmer
- No pets at home

**Health-Related Behaviors:**

- Non-smoker
- Denies alcohol and illicit drug use
- No recent tick bites, exposure to farm animals, or travel

**Allergies:**
No known drug allergies

**Review of Systems:**
Negative except as noted in HPI.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 97.7°F (36.5°C)
- Heart Rate: 88 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 133/88 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 12 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 98% on room air

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Alert, awake, in no acute distress
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Unremarkable
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rate and rhythm; normal heart sounds without murmurs, rubs, or gallops
- **Pulmonary:** Clear to auscultation bilaterally; no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi
- **Abdomen:** Soft, non-tender, non-distended; normal bowel sounds
- **Neurological:** Grossly intact; no focal deficits
- **Extremities/Skin:** No rashes noted on exposed skin. Slight redness observed on the right flank area. No tenderness over the ribs. No lesions or vesicles apparent.
