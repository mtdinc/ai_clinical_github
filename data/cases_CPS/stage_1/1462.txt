**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
A 69-year-old male presents with right lower back and buttock pain.

**History of Present Illness:**
The patient reports that the pain began seven weeks ago following an enema administered for diverticulitis. Over the past seven days, the pain has worsened, significantly limiting his mobility. He describes the pain as sharp, rated 7 out of 10, located in the right lower back, and radiating to the anterolateral region of the thigh. He notes numbness in the right lower quadrant and thigh. The pain intensifies during bowel movements and straining but is relieved by bending forward. He has experienced an unintended weight loss of 35 pounds, which he attributes to diverticulitis. He reports mild constipation related to pain medications and denies fever, chills, rash, kidney stones, or urinary issues.

**Past Medical History:**

- Shingles
- Diverticulitis
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
- Coronary artery disease (right coronary artery involvement)
- Obstructive sleep apnea (uses CPAP)
- Gastroesophageal reflux disease
- Osteoarthritis
- Benign prostatic hyperplasia
- Bilateral knee replacement

**Family History:**
Family history of gout.

**Social History:**

- Able to perform all activities of daily living.
- Engages in moderate physical activity and exercise.
- Tobacco use.
- Consumes approximately six beers per month.

**Medications:**

- Metoprolol
- Ketorolac

**Allergies:**
No known allergies.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: Normal
- Heart Rate: Normal
- Blood Pressure: Normal
- Respiratory Rate: Normal

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Lying flat on his back; alert and oriented.
- **HEENT:** Normal.
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rate and rhythm; normal S1 and S2 sounds.
- **Pulmonary:** Clear to auscultation bilaterally.
- **Abdominal:** Soft with tenderness in the right lower quadrant.
