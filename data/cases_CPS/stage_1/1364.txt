**Case Presentation – Stage 1**

**Chief Complaint:**

A 39-year-old male presents with painful mucosal necrotic lesions that began 10 days ago, accompanied by flu-like symptoms, fatigue, nausea, and body aches.

**History of Present Illness:**

Five days prior, the patient was presumed to have COVID-19 and was treated with steroids and clindamycin without improvement. He received a mouthwash for suspected candidiasis, which did not alleviate his symptoms. He reports difficulty swallowing, a non-painful penile ulcer with white discharge, and eye discharge.

**Past Medical History:**

- No history of hypertension.
- No history of type II diabetes mellitus.

**Family History:**

- Non-contributory.

**Medications:**

- Current acute medications: Steroids, clindamycin, acyclovir.

**Social History:**

- No recent travel history.
- Lives with two dogs.
- Occupation: High school teacher.
- Does not smoke tobacco.
- Denies contact with sick individuals.
- No recent changes in sexual partners.

**Health-Related Behaviors:**

- Non-smoker.
- No alcohol or illicit drug use mentioned.

**Allergies:**

- No known drug allergies.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 39.8°C
- Heart Rate: 114 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 113/79 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: Patient is breathing comfortably on room air.

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** The patient appears toxic.
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Bilateral conjunctivitis without itching or photophobia; multiple white spots on the oral mucosa and upper and lower lips.
- **Cardiovascular:** Tachycardia with regular rhythm.
- **Pulmonary:** Heavy cough; lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally.
- **Neurological:** No focal neurological deficits noted.
- **Extremities/Skin:** Skin is warm and dry; no skin ulcers observed on extremities; one ulcer present on the penile shaft.
