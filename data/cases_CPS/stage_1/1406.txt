**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
A 40-year-old man is referred to the emergency department for evaluation of high blood pressure.

**History of Present Illness:**
The patient reports several weeks of a non-productive cough and shortness of breath, particularly with exertion. He denies fever and other systemic symptoms.

**Review of Systems:**
- **General:** No fever, chills, or weight loss.
- **HEENT:** No visual changes or headaches.
- **Cardiovascular:** Reports shortness of breath on exertion.
- **Respiratory:** Non-productive cough.
- **Gastrointestinal:** Denies gastrointestinal symptoms.
- **Genitourinary:** No genitourinary symptoms.
- **Neurological:** No dizziness or weakness.

**Past Medical History:**
- Hypertension.

**Medications:**
- None.

**Allergies:**
- Denies any known drug allergies.

**Family History:**
- Several family members with high blood pressure and heart disease.

**Social History:**
- Does not use tobacco or alcohol.
- Reports occasional use of cocaine and marijuana.
- No history of surgeries.
- No recent travel history.

**Vital Signs:**
- Blood Pressure: 204/130 mm Hg.
- Heart Rate: 98 beats per minute.
- Respiratory Rate: 18 breaths per minute.
- Oxygen Saturation: 92% on room air.

**Physical Examination:**
- **General Appearance:** The patient appears calm and relaxed.
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Unremarkable.
- **Cardiovascular:** Tachycardic with regular rhythm.
- **Respiratory:** Normal breath sounds; no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi.
- **Abdomen:** Non-tender and distended; no hepatomegaly.
- **Extremities/Skin:** No edema; normal skin turgor.
