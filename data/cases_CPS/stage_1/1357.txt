**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

A 59-year-old female presents to the emergency department with shortness of breath for the past three days. One week prior, she experienced abdominal discomfort and visited a local clinic where an endoscopy was performed. She was diagnosed with gastritis and started on esomeprazole. Three days ago, she developed shortness of breath and noticed mild swelling in her lower extremities. She returned to the local clinic and was told she had cystitis; medication was prescribed but her symptoms did not improve.

She reports associated symptoms of nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and poor appetite. She denies headache, chest pain, orthopnea, fever, diarrhea, bloody stools, or melena.

Her past medical history is significant for a transcervical uterine myomectomy 15 years ago and a spontaneous abortion. Obstetric history: gravida 3, para 2, abortus 1; she has one daughter and one son. Family history includes diabetes mellitus in her mother and colon cancer in her father.
