**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 37-year-old male from Morocco presents with 4 weeks of muscle pain in his bilateral elbows, knees, and wrists without any associated swelling. The pain has not responded to nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). He reports no other symptoms.

- **Past Medical History:** None.
- **Family History:** Three brothers with chronic kidney disease (CKD) who are on dialysis.
- **Social History:** He has a 10 pack-year history of smoking.
- **Medications:** None.
- **Allergies:** None.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: Afebrile.
- Blood Pressure: Within normal limits.
- Heart Rate: Within normal limits.
- Respiratory Rate: Within normal limits.
- Oxygen Saturation: Within normal limits.

**Physical Examination:**

- **HEENT:** Notable for tinnitus and mild hearing loss.
- **Cardiovascular:** Heart sounds are normal with no murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
- **Pulmonary:** Lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally.
- **Abdominal:** Soft, non-tender, non-distended with normal bowel sounds.
- **Neurologic:** Alert and oriented to person, place, and time.
- **Musculoskeletal:** Reports polyarthralgias in large joints without visible swelling or redness.
- **Dermatologic:** Bilateral pitting edema is present, along with scattered petechiae on the torso.
