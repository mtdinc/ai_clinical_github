**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 59-year-old male presents with a two-week history of worsening bilateral lower extremity swelling and shortness of breath. He notes that his right leg is more swollen than the left. He works in a job that requires standing all day. He denies orthopnea and paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, stating he can lie flat without difficulty throughout the night. He reports increased difficulty climbing the two flights of stairs to his home, an activity he performs daily. He denies chest pain, palpitations, dizziness, syncope, weight loss, fatigue, or night sweats. He has not traveled outside Illinois.
