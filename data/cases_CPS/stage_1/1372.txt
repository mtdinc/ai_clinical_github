**Case Presentation**

---

**Stage 1**

A 45-year-old male presents to the emergency department with a three-day history of epigastric pain accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and heartburn. One day prior to admission, he developed sudden onset of left-sided facial asymmetry, dizziness, and gait imbalance.

**Past Medical History:**

- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (Hemoglobin A1c: 6.1%)
- Ischemic heart disease with a prior myocardial infarction
- Long-standing history of heartburn associated with nausea and vomiting

**Family History:**

- Non-contributory

**Social History:**

- Married
- Works as a manual laborer
- Denies tobacco use, alcohol consumption, and illicit drug use

**Medications:**

- Metformin
- Vildagliptin
- Dapagliflozin
- Clopidogrel
- Rosuvastatin
- Bisoprolol

**Allergies:**

- None reported

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: Within normal limits
- Heart Rate: Within normal limits
- Blood Pressure: Within normal limits
- Respiratory Rate: 18 breaths per minute

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Obese male, alert and oriented, in no acute distress
- **Cardiovascular:** Normal S1 and S2, regular rhythm, no murmurs
- **Respiratory:** Clear breath sounds bilaterally
- **Abdominal:** Tenderness in the epigastric region, no rebound or guarding
- **Neurological:**
  - **Mental Status:** Glasgow Coma Scale score of 15
  - **Cranial Nerves:**
    - Pupils equal and reactive to light
    - Facial asymmetry with mouth deviation to the left
    - Decreased hearing on the left to finger rub
    - Extraocular movements intact
  - **Motor:** Normal muscle tone and strength bilaterally
  - **Cerebellar:**
    - Bidirectional horizontal nystagmus with upbeating torsional component, worse on the left
    - Dysmetria and dysdiadochokinesia on the left
    - Failed finger-to-nose test on the left
    - Impaired heel-to-shin test on the left
    - Ataxic gait favoring the left side
  - **Sensory:** Intact sensation bilaterally
- **Other:** No skin rashes or lesions noted
