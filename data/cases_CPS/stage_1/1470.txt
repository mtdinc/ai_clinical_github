### **Stage 1**

**Chief Complaint:**

A 38-year-old male presents with shortness of breath for 20 days, swelling of both feet for 20 days, and easy fatigability for 30 days.

**History of Present Illness:**

The patient reports an insidious onset of shortness of breath that has gradually progressed, rendering him unable to perform minimal activities such as dressing. He denies nocturnal awakening due to dyspnea and does not experience difficulty breathing while lying down. The bilateral lower extremity swelling began 20 days ago, extending up to the knees. The edema does not subside with rest but improved after taking oral furosemide.

Approximately 1.5 months ago, he noticed yellowish discoloration of his eyes lasting for one week, unaccompanied by itching or pale-colored stools, which resolved after taking ursodeoxycholic acid. He experienced blackish discoloration of stools 2-3 times per day for three days, describing the stools as sticky and malodorous. He also had episodes of bleeding gums lasting for two days, which subsided without intervention.

**Past Medical History:**

- Hypothyroidism (poor compliance with medication)
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

**Social History:**

- Smokes 2-3 cigarettes per day
- Alcohol use disorder for 8 years, consuming 90-180 ml of brandy daily
- Chews tobacco
- No illicit drug use reported

**Medications:**

- Thyroxine 50mg daily (poor compliance)
- Metformin 500mg daily

**Allergies:**

- No known drug allergies

**Family History:**
