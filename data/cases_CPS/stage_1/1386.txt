### **Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

#### **Chief Complaint:**
A 94-year-old woman presents with her first episode of a tonic-clonic seizure.

#### **History of Present Illness:**
The patient was in her usual state of health until the previous day when she experienced a sensation of leaving her body. During this episode, she became confused, had urinary incontinence (wet her bed), and exhibited jerking movements lasting approximately one minute. There was no prior history of seizures or similar events. She did not respond during the episode but regained awareness afterward.

#### **Past Medical History:**
- **Breast Cancer:** Right-sided invasive ductal carcinoma with metastases, currently being treated with anastrozole. Interval PET scans have shown a reduction in metastatic lesions.
- **Hypertension**
- **Hyperlipidemia**

#### **Medications:**
- Anastrozole
- Amlodipine
- Rosuvastatin

#### **Allergies:**
- No known drug allergies.

#### **Family History:**
- Maternal aunt had breast cancer.

#### **Social History:**
- Vietnamese origin; moved to the United States in 1980.
- Has traveled back to Vietnam four times.
- Does not smoke, consume alcohol, or use illicit substances.
- Eats meat regularly.
- No pets and no known contact with animals.

#### **Review of Systems:**
- Negative except as noted in the history of present illness.

#### **Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: 97.1°F (36.2°C)
- Heart Rate: 55 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 125/61 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: *Not provided*
- Oxygen Saturation: 95% on room air

#### **Physical Examination:**
- **General:** Well-appearing elderly woman in no acute distress.
- **Eyes:** Presence of a retinal cystic lesion.
- **Cardiovascular:** Normal rate and rhythm; no murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
- **Pulmonary:** Clear to auscultation bilaterally without wheezes, rales, or rhonchi.
- **Abdomen:** Soft, non-tender, non-distended; no hepatosplenomegaly.
- **Neurological:**
  - Alert and oriented to person, place, and time.
  - Cranial nerves II-XII intact.
  - Motor strength 5/5 in all extremities.
  - Coordination intact (normal finger-to-nose test).
  - Negative Romberg sign.
  - Sensation intact to light touch and pinprick in all extremities.
