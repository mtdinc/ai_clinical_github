Stage 1:**

A 63-year-old gentleman with a past medical history of type 2 diabetes mellitus, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia presents to the emergency department after his primary care physician noted a serum calcium level of 14 mg/dL during routine laboratory testing. He reports dealing with a cough over the last couple of weeks and experiencing mild dyspnea. Additionally, he mentions a decreased appetite and an unintended weight loss of 10 pounds. He has been constipated, with his last bowel movement occurring a week ago.

His medications include a statin, vitamin D 1000 units daily, lisinopril, and metformin. He denies any known drug allergies.

Social history is significant for tobacco use of half a pack of cigarettes per day for the past 40 years and consuming a six-pack of beer daily. He has not had episodes of alcohol withdrawal. Family history is non-contributory for malignancies.

Vital signs on presentation are:

- **Heart rate:** 68 beats per minute
- **Blood pressure:** 174/72 mmHg
- **Respiratory rate:** 16 breaths per minute

On physical examination:

- **General:** Well-developed gentleman without cachexia.
- **HEENT:** Normocephalic, atraumatic; no scleral icterus.
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rate and rhythm; no murmurs; no jugular venous distention.
- **Pulmonary:** No respiratory distress; clear to auscultation bilaterally.
- **Abdominal:** Mildly distended abdomen; no organomegaly appreciated.
- **Neurological:** Alert and oriented; noted poor memory recall.
- **Extremities/Skin:** Warm and dry skin; peripheral pulses are palpable and normal.
