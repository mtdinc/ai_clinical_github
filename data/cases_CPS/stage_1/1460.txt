**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**  
A 44-year-old female presents with two days of chest pressure.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient reports the onset of constant chest pressure two days ago while attending a music festival. She was walking in high heels when she first noticed the pressure in her chest, accompanied by shortness of breath and numbness in her left arm. The chest pressure resolves with rest but recurred this morning upon waking, now associated with notable diaphoresis and nausea. She denies any prior episodes of similar chest pressure. Four years ago, she had COVID-19 and has experienced persistent shortness of breath since then. She denies fever, chills, pain worsening with deep inspiration, positional variation, back pain, or difficulty swallowing.

**Past Medical History:**

- Asthma
- Pulmonary fibrosis complicated by chronic anemia
- Hemorrhoidectomy

**Medications:**  
None.

**Allergies:**  
No known drug allergies.

**Family History:**  
Non-contributory.

**Social History:**  
- Occupation: Works as a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA).
- Substance Use: Intermittent alcohol consumption, marijuana use, and daily vaping.

**Health-Related Behaviors:**  
Denies tobacco smoking. Engages in regular physical activity as part of her work duties.

**Review of Systems:**  
Negative except as noted in the history of present illness.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 36.6°C (97.9°F)
- Heart Rate: 63 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 111/75 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 18 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 100% on room air

**Physical Examination:**

- **General Appearance:** Well-appearing female, not in acute distress.
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Normocephalic, atraumatic. Pupils equal, round, reactive to light. Oropharynx moist without lesions.
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rate and rhythm (RRR); normal S1 and S2; no murmurs, rubs, or gallops detected.
- **Pulmonary:** Clear to auscultation bilaterally; no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi.
