**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
Abdominal pain and distension.

**History of Present Illness:**
A 61-year-old male with no known past medical history presents with a two-week history of worsening abdominal pain, nausea, and shortness of breath. The abdominal pain is described as cramping with significant distension. He reports fatigue, lower extremity edema, weakness, decreased appetite, and an unintentional weight loss of approximately 30-40 pounds over the past three months. He has experienced diarrhea with mucus during this period, which was somewhat alleviated by over-the-counter loperamide (Imodium).

Upon arrival at the emergency department, his blood pressure was 90/70 mmHg and heart rate was over 100 beats per minute. He received intravenous normal saline bolus and was admitted to the hospital for further evaluation and management.
