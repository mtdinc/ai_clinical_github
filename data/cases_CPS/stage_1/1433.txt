**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
A 58-year-old woman presents with left hip pain for 1 month.

**History of Present Illness:**
The patient reports a 1-month history of progressive, constant medial left hip pain. The pain worsens with walking and is associated with numbness, tingling, and shooting sensations radiating to the knee. She has noticed limping due to the pain. There was no preceding trauma. Additionally, she has experienced back pain for the past year, which has worsened over the past month. She denies abdominal pain, chills, weight loss, and shortness of breath.

Approximately one week after obtaining a hip X-ray, her condition deteriorated, and she now cannot walk without support, often needing to lean on walls for assistance.

**Past Medical History:**
- No significant medical history.

**Past Surgical History:**

- Knee surgery for meniscal tear.

**Family History:**

**Social History:**

- Works as a nurse.

**Medications:**

**Allergies:**
- No known drug allergies.
