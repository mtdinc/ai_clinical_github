**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
An 82-year-old man presents to the emergency department with fever and difficulty breathing.

**History of Present Illness:**
The patient reports that his fever began one week ago, accompanied by upper respiratory symptoms. He has a productive cough and notes that his shortness of breath worsens when lying down at night. He mentions experiencing episodes of hemoptysis (coughing up blood) and epistaxis (nosebleeds) 2 to 3 months prior to this presentation.

**Past Medical History:**
- Hypertension for 4 years
- Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) for 10 years
- Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
- Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
- Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)

**Medications:**
- Non-compliant with prescribed medications

**Family History:**
- Non-contributory

**Social History:**
- Former smoker with a 15 pack-year history; quit 10 years ago
- No alcohol use history
- Recent exposure to tuberculosis
- Denies illicit drug use

**Health-Related Behaviors:**
- Not specified

**Allergies:**
- None

**Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: 101°F (38.3°C)
- Heart Rate: 85 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 110/70 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 28 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 88% on room air

**Physical Examination:**

- **General Appearance:** The patient appears malnourished, pale, and is sweating. He exhibits labored breathing.
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Moist mucous membranes are noted with erythema; no lymphadenopathy is present.
- **Cardiovascular:** Normal heart sounds with no jugular venous distension.
- **Pulmonary:** Fine inspiratory crackles are heard at the lung bases, more prominent on the right than the left.
- **Abdomen:** Normal examination findings.
- **Neurological:** Alert and oriented with a Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) score of 15.
- **Extremities/Skin:** No rashes or lesions observed.
