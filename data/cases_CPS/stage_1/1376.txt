**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
A 51-year-old male presents to the emergency department after experiencing a psychiatric episode one day ago during which he undressed at work.

**History of Present Illness:**
The patient reports an episode of undressing at work, which is uncharacteristic for him. He denies loss of consciousness, behavioral changes (outside of the presenting incident), headaches, seizures, focal neurological deficits, gastrointestinal symptoms, urinary symptoms, or a history of psychiatric disorders. He has been out of regular medical care for several years.

**Past Medical History:**
- Out of regular medical care for several years.

**Medications:**
- None.

**Allergies:**
- No known drug allergies.

**Family History:**
- Non-contributory.

**Social History:**
- Born and raised in Brazil.
- Lives with his mother.
- Works at the airport.
- Owns a dog.
- No recent travel.
- Sexual history: Men who have sex with men (MSM).
- Denies tobacco, alcohol, and illicit drug use.

**Review of Systems:**
- Negative except as noted in the history of present illness.

**Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: Within normal limits.
- Heart Rate: Within normal range.
- Blood Pressure: Oscillating readings, reaching up to 150/100 mmHg.
- Respiratory Rate: Normal.

**Physical Examination:**
- **General:** Mild dehydration noted.
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Pupils equal, round, and reactive to light and accommodation (PERRLA).
- **Cardiovascular:** Normal heart sounds; no murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
- **Respiratory:** Clear to auscultation bilaterally; no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi.
- **Abdominal:** Soft, non-tender, non-distended; normal bowel sounds.
- **Neurological:**
  - Alert and oriented to place but not to time.
  - Confused and agitated with fluctuating mental status between delirium and periods of normalcy.
  - Short-term and long-term memory intact.
  - Cranial nerves II-XII intact.
  - Cerebellar function normal.
  - Coordination normal.
  - Deep tendon reflexes normal.
  - Bladder and bowel function normal.
- **Skin/Extremities:** No rashes or lesions; mild dehydration evident.
