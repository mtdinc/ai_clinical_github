**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

A 47-year-old male presents to the emergency department with a chief complaint of right-sided chest pain and shortness of breath that began a few hours ago immediately after landing from a flight. The chest pain is pleuritic in nature and accompanied by diaphoresis. He reports flying twice per month for his job as a sales representative. One week prior to presentation, he began experiencing bilateral leg cramping and admits to inadequate hydration. He denies any new or productive cough, presyncope, palpitations, fever, or chills.

**Past Medical History:**

- Urinary tract infection and sexually transmitted infection; both treated with antibiotics.

**Medications:**

- None.

**Allergies:**

- Rash after penicillin.

**Family History:**

- Mother had an intracerebral hemorrhage.
- Father had prostate cancer.

**Social History:**

- Smokes two cigarettes per day.
- Consumes one beer daily.
- Denies recreational drug use.
- Works as a sales representative.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: Afebrile.
- Heart Rate: 115 beats per minute.
- Blood Pressure: 152/105 mm Hg.
- Oxygen Saturation: 92% on 2 liters nasal cannula.

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Well-appearing, no acute distress, body mass index (BMI) of 38.
- **HEENT:** Unremarkable.
- **Cardiovascular:** Tachycardic, regular rhythm, no appreciable murmurs, jugular venous distention present.
- **Pulmonary:** Normal work of breathing, basilar rhonchi noted, speaks in short, fragmented sentences.
- **Abdomen:** Soft, mildly tender and distended across all four quadrants, no hepatosplenomegaly.
- **Neurological:** No focal deficits, moves all limbs symmetrically.
- **Extremities/Skin:** 2+ pitting edema bilaterally, no limitations in range of motion.
