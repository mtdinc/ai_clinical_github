

**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 65-year-old woman presents with a sudden onset of right-sided headache localized to the temporal and orbital areas, which began one week ago. She does not report any associated fever or congestion. One week prior, she experienced a "runny nose" and was treated for sinusitis. Despite treatment, the headache persisted, and she developed sudden onset of right-sided ptosis and double vision in all directions, without blurred vision. The headache is described as throbbing and persistent. She denies congestion, lacrimation, rhinorrhea, fever, anosmia, facial numbness or asymmetry, decreased hearing, dysphagia, slurred speech, or proptosis. There are no associated aura, nausea, vomiting, photophobia, or phonophobia. She has never experienced this type of headache before and denies any motor weakness or sensory disturbances.

Her past medical history is significant for hypertension and diabetes mellitus. She has no history of surgeries. Medications include amlodipine, metformin, and multivitamins. She has a 40-year history of smoking 4-5 cigars per day. She denies alcohol and illicit drug use. She is single and lives with her niece's family. She reports no known drug allergies.

**Vital Signs:**  
- Temperature: 97.4°F (36.3°C)  
- Heart Rate: 80 beats per minute  
- Blood Pressure: 140/90 mmHg  
- Respiratory Rate: 12 breaths per minute  
- Oxygen Saturation: 100% on room air  

**Physical Examination:**  
- **General, Pulmonary, Abdominal, Extremities:** Unremarkable.  
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular heart sounds with a mild systolic murmur at the aortic area; no gallops or rubs.  
- **Neurological Examination:**  
  - **Mental Status:** Alert and oriented to time, place, and person; able to recall 3/3 words after 5 minutes; normal language function.  
  - **Cranial Nerves:**  
    - **CN I:** Sense of smell intact.  
    - **CN II:** Pupils equal and reactive to light; no relative afferent pupillary defect.  
    - **CN III, IV, VI:** Right ptosis; right eye movement restricted in all directions; double vision reported.  
    - **CN V1:** Decreased pinprick sensation on the right side.  
    - **CN V2, V3, VII, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII:** Normal findings.  
  - **Motor and Sensory:** Intact vibration and proprioception; no motor weakness.  
  - **Cerebellar Function:** No gait disturbances; no nystagmus.
