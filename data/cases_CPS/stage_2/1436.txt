**Stage 1 Case Presentation:**

A 54-year-old female presents with a 2-week history of left-sided chest pain.

**History of Present Illness:**
- The chest pain is mild in intensity, sharp and stabbing in quality, non-radiating, and unchanged with exertion or position.
- She reports a productive cough with brownish sputum for the past month, accompanied by worsening shortness of breath during this time.
- She experiences diffuse body pain at baseline due to fibromyalgia.
- Denies fevers, chills, orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (PND), lower extremity edema, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, night sweats, weight loss, or confusion.

**Past Medical History:**
- **Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)/Asthma** for 15 years; last exacerbation 2 months ago.
- **Fibromyalgia.**
- **Ankylosing Spondylitis;** last dose of adalimumab was 2 months ago.
- **Tuberculous Sacroiliitis** treated with 9 months of RIPE therapy in 2011.
- **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)** for 5 years, managed conservatively.

**Medications:**
- Fluticasone-salmeterol inhaler.
- Duloxetine.

**Social History:**
- Originally from El Salvador; immigrated to Chicago in 1998.
- No recent travel.
- No pets or exposure to sick contacts.
- Smoked 1 pack per day for 30 years; quit 2 years ago.
- Denies alcohol and illicit drug use.

**Family History:**
- Not provided.

**Allergies:**
- Not provided.

**Review of Systems:**
- As per history of present illness.

**Physical Examination:**
- **Vital Signs:** Temperature, heart rate, blood pressure, and respiratory rate are within normal limits.
- **General:** Appears comfortable.
- **Pulmonary:** Reduced breath sounds over the left lung base; diffuse wheezing throughout.
- **Cardiovascular:** Normal heart sounds without murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
- **Abdomen:** Soft, non-tender, non-distended.
- **Neurological:** Alert and oriented; normal mentation.
- **Extremities/Skin:** No edema or skin abnormalities noted.

**Stage 2 Case Presentation:**

**Laboratory Data:**

- **Complete Blood Count (CBC):**
  - **White Blood Cell Count:** 11,000/mm³ (neutrophil predominant, no eosinophilia).
  - **Hemoglobin:** 13.3 g/dL.
  - **Mean Corpuscular Volume:** 90 fL.
  - **Platelet Count:** 353,000/mm³.

- **Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP):** Within normal limits.
- **Liver Function Tests (LFTs):** Within normal limits.
- **Troponin:** Within normal limits.
- **Coagulation Profile:** Prothrombin time (PT), partial thromboplastin time (PTT), and international normalized ratio (INR) are within normal limits.
- **Urinalysis:**
  - Within normal limits; no proteinuria or cellular abnormalities detected.

**Imaging Studies:**

- **Chest X-Ray (CXR):**
  - Small effusion in the left basal aspect of the lung.
  - Hazy opacities observed in the left lower lung field.

- **Computed Tomography (CT) Scan of the Chest:**
  - Small left lower lobe subsegmental pulmonary embolism without evidence of right heart strain.
  - Left lower lobe consolidation with air bronchograms.
  - Small pleural effusion; unsuccessful attempt at thoracentesis.

**Hospital Course (Days 1-3):**

- **Day 1-2:**
  - The patient becomes progressively hypoxemic, requiring high-flow nasal cannula at 60 L/min with 90% FiO₂ to maintain oxygen saturation of 92%.
  - Point-of-care ultrasound shows unchanged pleural effusion size.
  - Initiated on intravenous ceftriaxone and azithromycin for suspected pneumonia.

- **Day 3:**
  - Transferred to the Intensive Care Unit due to worsening respiratory status.
  - Started on hydrocortisone 50 mg every 6 hours for suspected underlying inflammation.
  - Receives hydromorphone, ketorolac, and morphine for pain management.
  - Despite interventions, the patient's condition continues to deteriorate.
