**Stage 1 Case Presentation:**

A 30-year-old woman presents with bilateral limb pain and headache. Over the past three months, she has experienced gradually progressive calf pain and increasing difficulty walking, now limited to 100 meters before needing to rest. Concurrently, she reports a diffuse, dull-aching headache without any aggravating factors. She denies photophobia, phonophobia, fever, rash, joint pain, or recent pet exposure. Additionally, she notes blurry vision but can perceive colors well, with no diplopia or pain during eye movements.

Her past medical history includes an episode of acute pancreatitis with a negative workup for gallstones, drug-induced causes, calcium abnormalities, and triglyceride levels. She has no known family history of medical conditions and denies any significant social history, including tobacco, alcohol, or illicit drug use. She is not currently taking any medications and has no known allergies.

**Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: Not recorded
- Pulse: 76 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: Upper limbs unrecordable due to feeble peripheral pulses; lower limb systolic blood pressure is 70 mmHg

**Physical Examination:**
- **General:** Presence of xanthelasma; well-developed woman in no acute distress
- **Cardiovascular:** Normal heart sounds without murmurs
- **Pulmonary:** Fine crackles heard bilaterally
- **Abdominal:** Hepatosplenomegaly noted on palpation
- **Neurological:** Grade 3 papilledema observed; no focal neurological deficits
- **Extremities/Skin:** Absent peripheral pulses in the upper and lower limbs
