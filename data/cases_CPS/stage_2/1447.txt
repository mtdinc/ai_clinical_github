**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 76-year-old female presents with encephalopathy for one week. She is nonverbal due to voice hoarseness and has experienced altered mental status, progressive confusion, and fatigue over the past week. She reports mild weight loss over the past month. She denies abdominal pain, reflux, chest pain, shortness of breath, leg swelling, fever, chills, rash, or headache. She recently had a gastrointestinal illness with nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Her past medical history includes anxiety, situs inversus, and recurrent falls. Surgical history is significant for cholecystectomy and fallopian tube removal. Her only medication is oxazepam 10mg taken for anxiety. Family history reveals hypertension in her brother and hypertension and hyperlipidemia in her sister; there is no history of cancer. She is a retired clerk who lives alone.

In terms of health-related behaviors, she has a long-standing history of heavy smoking (two packs per day) and consumes 3–4 alcoholic drinks per day. She denies illicit drug use and recent travel. She has no known drug allergies.
