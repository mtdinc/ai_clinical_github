**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

A 53-year-old male presents to the emergency department complaining of pain under his jaw that began one hour prior to arrival. The pain is described as crushing in nature, does not radiate, is not induced by effort, and does not respond to painkillers. It remains constant regardless of position and is unaffected by both passive and active movements of the jaw throughout the entire range of motion. There is no erythema or tenderness around the jaw joint.

He denies experiencing nausea, vomiting, dyspnea, chest pain, headache, facial pain, or claudication.
