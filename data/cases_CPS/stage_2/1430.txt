**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
Left-sided weakness.

**History of Present Illness:**
A 48-year-old right-handed woman presents with sudden onset of left-sided weakness that began 4 hours prior to admission. The weakness affected both her left arm and leg and has remained constant since onset. She also reports difficulty with speech (dysarthria). She denies dysphagia, vertigo, blurred vision, diplopia, dysphonia, nausea, or vomiting.

She notes that four months ago, she began experiencing episodic right-sided weakness and facial paresis accompanied by dysarthria. These episodes resolved spontaneously within a day. She sought medical attention at another hospital but had recurrent attacks prior to admission. No interventions were noted from the prior evaluations.
