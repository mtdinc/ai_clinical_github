**Case Presentation - Stage 1**

A 51-year-old male presents to the emergency department with right eye pain and blurred vision for the past three days. He reports associated visual loss in the right eye. The eye is red, swollen, and appears to be protruding. He notes having fevers during this period. He denies pain with eye movement, trauma, sick contacts, medication changes, or any prior history of eye issues. He has experienced mild headaches, palpitations, light-headedness, and reports profound diarrhea and nausea.

**Past Medical History:**

- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (on insulin)
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
- Obstructive Sleep Apnea
- Ulcerative Colitis (with a recent negative colonoscopy)

**Medications:**

- Vedolizumab
- Apixaban
- Mesalamine
- Insulin Glargine
- Atorvastatin
- Fexofenadine

**Allergies:** Shellfish

**Family History:** Non-contributory

**Social History:**

- Retired Navy serviceman; previously worked in IT
- Tobacco use
- Denies alcohol and illicit drug use

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 101°F (38.3°C)
- Heart Rate: 135 beats per minute (irregular)
- Blood Pressure: 95/55 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 18 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 98% on room air

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Notably diaphoretic
- **HEENT:**
  - Right eye: Purulent discharge, conjunctival erythema, decreased vision, mild pain on lateral gaze
  - Left eye: Normal
  - Extraocular movements: Intact
- **Cardiovascular:** Tachycardic and irregular rhythm; no murmurs; no jugular venous distension
- **Pulmonary:** Clear breath sounds bilaterally; no respiratory distress
- **Abdomen:** Soft, non-tender; no organomegaly
- **Neurological:** Withdrawn but fully oriented; pupils equal and reactive; no focal deficits
- **Skin:** Warm and well-perfused; no edema or rash
