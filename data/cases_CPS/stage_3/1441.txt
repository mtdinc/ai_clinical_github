**Stage 1 Case Presentation:**

A 42-year-old female presents to the emergency department with a 2-3 day history of fever, myalgias, and body aches. She reports that she initially visited her primary care physician, where she was diagnosed with a viral syndrome after negative influenza and COVID-19 tests. She was discharged with symptomatic treatment but has experienced no improvement. Her symptoms have since worsened, including increased myalgias, cough, and headache. She denies any visual changes or sick contacts.

**Medical History:**

- **Past Medical History:**
  - Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (diagnosed at age 20): Manifestations include rash, arthralgias, serositis, and pleuritis.
  - Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease.

- **Surgical History:**
  - Appendectomy at age 14.

- **Medications:**
  - Azathioprine
  - Hydroxychloroquine
  - Prednisone
  - Proton Pump Inhibitor

- **Family History:**
  - Breast cancer in a relative.

- **Social History:**
  - Works at a salad bar.
  - Denies alcohol or other substance use.
  - Recent travel to Saudi Arabia five months prior.

- **Allergies:**
  - Penicillin (rash).

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 39.5°C
- Heart Rate: 110 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 110/60 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 24 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 92% on room air

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Ill-appearing woman.
- **Head and Neck:** Normal pharynx, no jaundice.
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rhythm, normal S1 and S2, no murmurs.
- **Pulmonary:** Lungs clear to auscultation bilaterally.
- **Abdominal:** Mild tenderness in the right lower quadrant; abdomen is soft with no costovertebral angle tenderness.
- **Skin:** Diffuse blanching rash over the entire body, sparing the palms and soles; some areas are tender.
- **Neurological:** Alert and oriented to person, place, and time; mild pain with neck flexion.
