**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 28-year-old female presented to the emergency department with a chief complaint of abdominal pain lasting for two weeks. The history is primarily obtained from her mother due to the patient's altered mental status. The abdominal pain is described as sharp, initially located around the umbilical region and later migrating to the suprapubic area. She reports associated nausea and episodes of bilious vomiting, with the most recent episode occurring this morning. She denies any vaginal discharge or history of sexually transmitted infections.
