**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**
A 58-year-old female presents to the clinic complaining of cough and shortness of breath.

**History of Present Illness:**
The patient reports experiencing fatigue and a dry cough for the past six months. Approximately three months ago, she noticed increasing tiredness and has become progressively unable to go for her usual long walks. Her shortness of breath has become more frequent and sometimes occurs even at rest. She denies any fever, chest pain, or vomiting.

**Past Medical History:**
- The patient has not seen a doctor in eight years.
- No known history of hypertension or diabetes.
- She mentions being diagnosed with rheumatoid arthritis eight years ago but is not on any treatment and provides vague details about the diagnosis.

**Medications:**
- None.

**Family History:**
- No significant family medical history reported.

**Social History:**
- Lives in Texas.
- Retired.
- Smoked cigarettes for the past 15 years.
- Consumes alcohol occasionally.
- No recent travel history.
- Denies exposure to animals.

**Review of Systems:**
- No additional symptoms reported beyond those in the history of present illness.

**Vital Signs:**
- Within normal limits.

**Physical Examination:**
- **General:** Patient appears not acutely ill and is able to converse and express concerns appropriately.
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Examination within normal limits.
- **Cardiovascular:** Normal heart sounds; no murmurs detected.
- **Respiratory:** Inspiratory rales (crackles) heard bilaterally.
- **Extremities/Skin:** Notable digital clubbing of fingers on both hands. Lower extremities appear normal.
