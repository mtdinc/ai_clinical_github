**Case Presentation - Stage 1**

---

**Chief Complaint:**

A 63-year-old male presents with diffuse myalgias, arthralgias, and chills.

---

**History of Present Illness:**

The patient reports the onset of arthralgias affecting his wrists and knees, leading to difficulty extending these joints and an inability to walk due to pain. He denies fever, night sweats, hematuria, abdominal pain, and bowel dysfunction.

---

**Past Medical History:**

- No known medical conditions.

---

**Medications:**

- None.

---

**Allergies:**

- None reported.

---

**Family History:**

- Not provided.

---

**Social History:**

- Recent history of multiple tick bites during a hiking trip in Maryland.
- Denies tobacco use, alcohol consumption, and illicit drug use.

---

**Health-Related Behaviors:**

- Does not smoke.
- Abstains from alcohol.
- No use of recreational drugs.

---

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 100.4°F (38°C)
- Heart Rate: 80 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 120/80 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 23 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 93% on room air

---

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Alert and oriented to person, place, and time.
- **Cardiovascular:** Heart sounds normal; regular rate and rhythm.
- **Pulmonary:** Clear breath sounds bilaterally.
- **Skin:** Capillary refill less than 2 seconds.

**Case Presentation - Stage 3**


**Hospital Course and Additional Findings:**

The patient was initiated on intravenous vancomycin and ceftriaxone for antimicrobial therapy. Swelling improved after two doses of corticosteroids. A detailed musculoskeletal examination revealed extreme pain and guarding with right hip flexion, abduction, and adduction, noted only after steroid administration.

**Advanced Imaging:**

- **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) and Computed Tomography (CT) of the Lumbar Spine and Abdomen:**
  - **Findings:**
    - L4-L5 epidural abscess.
    - Osteomyelitis at the L4-L5 vertebral levels.
    - Left psoas muscle abscess.

**Microbiology:**

- Cultures obtained from the abscess grew MRSA positive.
