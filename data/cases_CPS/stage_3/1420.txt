**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 43-year-old man presents with a two-week history of non-radiating periumbilical abdominal pain, loss of appetite, and diarrhea. He reports 5-6 episodes of yellow-brown stools daily, which occur irrespective of food intake. Over the past 2-3 days, he has noticed occasional frank hematochezia and recalls possible melena earlier in the course. He denies nausea, vomiting, or dyspepsia. He experiences myalgias and arthralgias, particularly in his knees and legs, along with intermittent numbness in his distal extremities. He endorses fever and fatigue but denies weight loss. Additionally, he notes "black urine" without accompanying flank pain, dysuria, or gross hematuria. Three days prior to presentation, he was seen at an urgent care clinic and was newly diagnosed with diabetes mellitus, hypertension, and hepatitis C.
