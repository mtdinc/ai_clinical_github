**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

**Chief Complaint:**

A 66-year-old male presents to the emergency department with a 10-day history of cough and fever.

**History of Present Illness:**

The patient reports a dry, non-productive cough that has been progressively worsening over the past 10 days. He experiences fatigue, fever reaching up to 38.5°C, diffuse myalgias, and exertional dyspnea. The shortness of breath has intensified to the extent that minimal activities, such as going to the toilet, trigger dyspnea; this was not present before the onset of symptoms. He was previously active, regularly engaging in biking and walking. He lives at home with his partner, who is asymptomatic. He denies chest pain, pleuritic pain, gastrointestinal symptoms (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal pain), arthralgias, or rash. There is no history of recent travel.
