**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 48-year-old female presented to the emergency department with fever, confusion, and a rash. She had been feeling well until five days prior when she rapidly developed fatigue, nausea, diffuse body aches, headache, and a subjective fever. She denies chest pain, shortness of breath, cough, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and urinary symptoms. There is no history of recent travel. Her partner reports episodes of intermittent confusion over the past few days.

**Past Medical History:** None.

**Medications:** None.

**Social History:**
- Lives on a farm in New Mexico with her partner.
- Exposed to cows, sheep, and a dog.
- Frequently outdoors and goes hunting in the forest.
- Does not recall any tick or mosquito bites.

**Health-Related Behaviors:**
- Does not consume alcohol.
- No history of substance abuse.

**Allergies:** None reported.

**Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: 39.1°C (102.4°F)
- Heart Rate: 110 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 110/70 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 25 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 96% on 2 liters nasal cannula

**Physical Examination:**

- **General Appearance:** Lethargic and diaphoretic.
- **Head, Eyes, Ears, Nose, Throat (HEENT):** Slight neck rigidity and stiffness; assessment for meningismus limited due to whole-body pain.
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rate and rhythm; no murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
- **Pulmonary:** Clear to auscultation bilaterally; no wheezes, rales, or rhonchi.
- **Abdominal:** Soft, non-tender, non-distended; normal bowel sounds.
- **Neurological:**
  - Disoriented to time and place but oriented to person.
  - Slow speech but able to follow instructions adequately.
  - No dysarthria or aphasia.
  - Pupils equal, round, and reactive to light.
  - No focal neurological deficits observed.
- **Skin/Extremities:**
  - Warm to touch.
  - Normal muscle strength and reflexes.
  - Notable for a reticular, non-blanchable, non-palpable rash that started on the abdomen and has since spread.
