**Stage 1 Case Presentation:**

A 28-year-old woman presents with a 2-3 day history of fever, lethargy, and tachypnea. One week prior, she developed a sore throat for which she received three days of ceftriaxone for presumed strep throat or tonsillitis but did not complete the antibiotic course after being lost to follow-up. Due to decreased level of consciousness, she was transferred to a tertiary care center. She reports neck pain, dysphagia, and dyspnea. She has experienced non-bloody vomiting over the past day. She denies chills, night sweats, weight loss, and states she felt well until one week ago. Review of systems is negative for headache, vision changes, chest pain, palpitations, cough, abdominal pain, diarrhea, hematochezia, melena, urinary symptoms, arthralgia, arthritis, and oral sores.
