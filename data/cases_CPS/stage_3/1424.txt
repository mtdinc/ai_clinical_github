**Stage 1: Initial Patient Presentation**

A 56-year-old male is referred to the emergency department with a suspicion of methanol toxicity. He experienced a loss of consciousness (LOC) three days ago after consuming moonshine at work and had another LOC after consuming moonshine last night. This morning, he passed out at his office desk. He reports episodes of diplopia and vertigo. His memory is intact, although events from the last few days are hazy. His brother confirms the timeline of events. He had a common cold last week but denies nausea, vomiting, or any recent trauma.

- **Past Medical History:** None reported.
- **Medications:** Thiamine and Vitamin B12 supplementation.
- **Family History:** Non-contributory.
- **Social History:** Office worker with no recent travel history.
- **Health-Related Behaviors:** Smokes half a pack per day for the past six months. History of chronic alcohol consumption, including homemade moonshine.
- **Allergies:** No known allergies.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: Afebrile
- Heart Rate: 88 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 130/80 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 18 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 99% on room air

**Physical Examination:**
