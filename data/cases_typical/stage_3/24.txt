**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 64-year-old male with diabetes is brought to the emergency department by his daughter who found him "not being himself" during their weekly dinner. She reports he was picking at his food, which is unusual, and seemed confused about what day it was. She noticed he was wearing the same clothes as yesterday and his house was unusually messy. The patient complains only of "feeling tired" and having some pain in his feet that's been "a little worse lately."

**Past Medical History:**
- Type 2 Diabetes (poorly controlled)
- Peripheral Neuropathy
- Chronic Kidney Disease Stage 3
- Hypertension
- Depression (recently widowed 8 months ago)

**Medications:**
- Metformin 1000mg twice daily
- Gabapentin 300mg three times daily
- Lisinopril 20mg daily
- Sertraline 50mg daily
- "Over-the-counter cream" for foot rash

**Social History:**
- Retired postal worker
- Lives alone since wife's death
- Daughter visits weekly
- No alcohol or tobacco
- Poor medication compliance recently
- Decreased oral intake past few days

**Vital Signs:**
- Temperature: 36.5°C
- Heart Rate: 112 beats/minute
- Blood Pressure: 102/58 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 22 breaths/minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 94% on room air

**Physical Examination:**
- **General:** Elderly male appearing older than stated age, lethargic but arousable
- **HEENT:** Dry mucous membranes
- **Cardiovascular:** Tachycardic, regular rhythm
- **Respiratory:** Scattered crackles at bases
- **Neurological:**
  - Confused to time
  - Otherwise grossly intact
- **Extremities:**
  - Right foot with dark discoloration between 4th and 5th toes
  - Surrounding erythema extending to ankle
  - Decreased sensation bilaterally
- **Skin:** Poor skin turgor
