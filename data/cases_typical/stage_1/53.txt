**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 30-year-old male graduate student presents to the emergency department with a three-day history of worsening headaches and fatigue. Initially, he attributed these symptoms to stress and sleep deprivation due to preparations for his thesis defense. Over the past day, he has noticed intermittent nausea and had two episodes of vomiting. He reports difficulty concentrating and feeling mentally "foggy." His girlfriend mentions that he seems more irritable and has been avoiding bright lights, preferring to stay in a dark room. He denies fever, neck stiffness, or recent illnesses. He has no history of migraines and states that this headache feels different from any he's had before.
