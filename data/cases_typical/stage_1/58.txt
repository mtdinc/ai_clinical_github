**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 16-year-old male high school student presents to the emergency department with acute onset of lower abdominal pain and nausea that began approximately 4 hours ago. He describes the pain as a deep, aching discomfort localized to the lower left quadrant of his abdomen. He rates the pain as 7 out of 10 and notes that it started suddenly while he was playing soccer during gym class. He initially thought it was a muscle strain but became more concerned as the pain persisted and worsened.

He reports feeling nauseated and has vomited once since the pain began. He denies any diarrhea, constipation, fever, or urinary symptoms such as dysuria or increased frequency. He also denies any recent trauma to the abdomen or groin area.

His past medical history is unremarkable. He has no chronic medical conditions and is up to date with his immunizations. He is not currently taking any medications and has no known drug allergies.

**Social History:**

- Lives with parents and has one younger sibling.
- Active in sports, particularly soccer and basketball.
- Denies alcohol, tobacco, or illicit drug use.
- Not sexually active.

**Family History:**

- Father: Kidney stones at age 40.
- Mother: Healthy.
- Sibling: Healthy.
- No family history of gastrointestinal or genitourinary diseases.

**Vital Signs:**

- Temperature: 37.1°C
- Heart Rate: 85 beats per minute
- Blood Pressure: 120/70 mmHg
- Respiratory Rate: 16 breaths per minute
- Oxygen Saturation: 99% on room air

**Physical Examination:**

- **General:** Alert and cooperative adolescent male, appears uncomfortable.
- **HEENT:** Normocephalic, atraumatic; mucous membranes moist.
- **Neck:** Supple, no lymphadenopathy.
- **Cardiovascular:** Regular rhythm; heart sounds normal with no murmurs.
- **Respiratory:** Clear breath sounds bilaterally.
- **Abdomen:** Soft with mild tenderness in the lower left quadrant; no rebound tenderness or guarding; bowel sounds present and normal.
- **Genitourinary:** Examination deferred at this time per patient comfort.
- **Musculoskeletal:** Full range of motion in all extremities; no tenderness or deformities.
- **Neurological:** Alert and oriented; cranial nerves II–XII intact; motor and sensory functions normal.
- **Skin:** Warm and dry; no rashes or lesions.
