**Stage 1: Initial Presentation**

A 42-year-old male presents to the emergency department with chest discomfort that started two days ago. He describes the pain as a constant, sharp ache located in the central chest area, sometimes radiating to the left shoulder. The pain is moderately severe, rated 6 out of 10, and worsens when he takes deep breaths or lies flat. He reports increasing shortness of breath over the past day. There is no history of trauma, exertion, or heavy lifting.

He mentions having a low-grade fever over the past three days, accompanied by general malaise and a mild dry cough without sputum production. He denies nausea, vomiting, excessive sweating, or palpitations.
